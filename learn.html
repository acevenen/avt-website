Mindset & Emotional Discipline

open ↓
Pillar 1 — Video lesson (add your Loom/YouTube link)

Most people don't fail trading because they couldn't find a setup. They fail because they couldn't handle the emotion of being wrong. Tilt, revenge trading, FOMO, ego — these kill funded accounts faster than any bad entry.

The four killers

  • Tilt — Taking a loss badly and immediately re-entering without a setup. You're not trading anymore, you're reacting.
  • Revenge trading — Doubling size after a loss to "get it back." The market doesn't owe you anything. You're now at 2x risk while emotional.
  • FOMO — Entering late because a move is already in motion. You're chasing. Your entry is garbage and you know it.
  • Overtrading — Taking 6 trades when your system only gave you 2 setups. More trades ≠ more money. It means more exposure to randomness.

The real edge

Discipline is the strategy. A perfect setup executed badly is a loss. A mediocre setup executed with full discipline is often a win. Your job is to follow the rules, not outsmart the market.

How to build the habit

  • Set a daily loss limit and treat it like a circuit breaker — once hit, session over. No exceptions.
  • If you take two losses back to back, step away for 15 minutes minimum before re-entering.
  • Journal every trade. Not just the P&L — how you felt before and after the entry. Patterns will emerge.
  • Ask after every trade: "Did I follow my rules?" Not "Did I make money?" A disciplined losing trade is still a win.

Ace's rule

If I wouldn't take the trade fresh and fully calm, I don't take it. Emotional state is part of the entry criteria.

Foundational Rules

open ↓
Pillar 2 — Video lesson (add your Loom/YouTube link)

This is the math before the chart. You can have the best setup in the world — if your risk management is broken, you will blow up. These are non-negotiables. Not guidelines. Not suggestions.

The non-negotiables

Instrument

NQ Futures (Nasdaq 100)

Highly liquid. Predictable volatility. Clean technical structure. Don't add instruments until you've mastered one.

Session

NY Open 9:30–11:30 AM EST

Highest volume, cleanest moves. Outside this window the setup probability drops significantly.

Stop Loss

Always set before entry

No SL = no trade. This is the rule that keeps you in the game when you're wrong.

Risk / Reward

2:1 minimum · 3:1 target

You can be wrong 40% of the time and still be profitable at 2:1. The math works for you, not against you.

Risk per trade

1–2% of account max

Prop firm accounts (Topstep, Lucid) typically have 2–4% daily loss limits. Stay well inside them.

The math — how it plays out

Scenario Trades Win rate Result
2:1 RR, average trader 10 50% +5R profit
2:1 RR, bad week 10 40% +2R profit
No RR, no system 10 60% −2R loss

Why most people fail

They take 1:1 trades at best, let losers run and cut winners early. Even with a 60% win rate, the math kills them. Fix the ratio first. Everything else is secondary.

Before every trade — quick checklist

SL is placed and I know the exact dollar risk
TP is at least 2x my SL distance
I'm trading NQ during NY open hours
This trade risks 2% of account or less
I'm not in a revenge/FOMO state

Reading Price Action

open ↓
Pillar 3 — Price action intro (add your Loom/YouTube link)

Before you can trade a setup, you need to speak the language of the chart. This is the vocabulary — what price is actually telling you, candle by candle.

Reading candles

Bullish — closed higher than it opened. Buyers in control.

Bearish — closed lower than it opened. Sellers in control.

The wicks tell you where price was rejected. A long wick = failed attempt to push further. That rejection is information.

Market structure

  • Higher Highs / Higher Lows (HH/HL) — Uptrend confirmed. Each pullback creates a higher low before pushing to a new high. Buy the dip.
  • Lower Highs / Lower Lows (LH/LL) — Downtrend confirmed. Each bounce gets rejected lower. Short the bounce.
  • Break of Structure (BoS) — When a swing high or low is taken out. This is often the start of a new move or a signal a reversal is forming.

Supply & Demand zones

  • Supply zone (resistance) — Area where price moved down aggressively from. Sellers were there before, likely still there.
  • Demand zone (support) — Area where price moved up aggressively from. Buyers were there before, likely still there.
  • The more times a zone is tested and holds, the more significant it becomes. But too many tests = weakening zone.
